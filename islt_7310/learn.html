Let's Learn Page

Goal 1

My first goal is to help each learner reach their potential. When it comes to your first job and teaching a concept, I feel that it can be very daunting. Just downright overwhelming. I know that I sure was overwhelmed when I first glanced at my curriculum mapping for the year. It is not something taught when studying to go into education. Or, in my case I haven’t enrolled in a class yet to teach me that. I feel like we learn about all different types of learners but we aren’t taught how to actually teach them a concept. I hope that my website can do that for you.

Goal 2

My second goal for my learners is to get them prepared for what they are teaching. Study the curriculum, look through it before you teach it to your students. There may be parts you want to change to fit your students better. There may be things you want to implement. Make sure that it fits somewhere within your teaching standards. As a first year teacher this is very important. It gets you prepared for the job you signed up to do. As the years go on you will know it like the back of your hand. Until then be prepared.

Goal 3

My third goal is to give my learners ideas on how to introduce a concept. There are many ways to introduce a concept to where it is exciting for your learners. Some lessons may already come with a plan on what to do but, if you are creating your own it can be a little daunting. Here are 4 ways that I introduce a concept in my classroom. Introduce with audio or video. This works great for any grade level in my opinion. Introduce with pictures, illustration or artifacts. Introduce with poetry, or a book. I’ve learned this works very well for my age group. Lastly introduce with a challenge or question. From my experience this really gets them intrigued.
